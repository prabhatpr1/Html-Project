The Perfect Crispy Samosa Recipe

This recipe yields approximately 10-12 medium-sized samosas.

1. Samosa Dough (Patti)

The secret to a flaky crust is using enough fat (ghee/oil) and kneading a stiff dough.

Ingredient Quantity
All-Purpose Flour (Maida) 2 Cups
Ghee or Oil (melted) 1/4 cup (4 cup)
Salt 1/2 tsp
Carom Seeds (Ajwain) 1/2 tsp (optional, but recommended)
Cold Water Approx. 1/2 cup

Instructions:

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients:In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and carom seeds (if using).
  2. Rub in the Fat (Moyan):Add the melted ghee or oil. Use your fingertips to rub the fat into the flour. Rub thoroughly for 3-4 minutes until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs. Test the mixture by pressing a handful—it should hold its shape.
  3. Knead the Dough:Add cold water, one tablespoon at a time. Mix until the dough comes together. Do NOT over-knead. The dough must be stiff and firm (unlike roti or chapati dough, which is soft). It should feel slightly rough and not smooth.
  4. Rest:Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30-45 minutes at room temperature. This is crucial for flakiness.

2. Spiced Potato Filling (Aloo Masala)

Ingredient Quantity
Potatoes (boiled, peeled, and cubed) 3-4 large
Green Peas (fresh or frozen) 1/2 cup
Oil 2 tbsp
Cumin Seeds (Jeera) 1 tsp
Ginger (finely chopped or grated) 1 tsp
Green Chilies (chopped) 1-2 (adjust to taste)
Coriander Powder 1 tsp
Garam Masala 1/2 tsp
Dry Mango Powder (Amchur) 1/2 tsp
Salt To taste
Fresh Coriander Leaves (chopped) 2 tbsp

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Potatoes: Boil the potatoes until cooked through, but not mushy. Peel them, then roughly mash or cube them. You want texture, not a smooth paste
  2. Sauté Spices: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add the chopped ginger and green chilies; sauté for 30 seconds.
  3. Add Peas & Dry Spices: Add the green peas (thaw first if frozen) and cook for 1 minute. Add the coriander powder, garam masala, and red chili powder. Cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Combine Filling: Add the potatoes and salt. Mix gently but thoroughly, making sure the spices coat the potatoes. Stir in the dry mango powder (Amchur) and fresh coriander leaves.
  5. Cool: Transfer the filling to a plate and let it cool completely before assembling the samosas.

3. Shaping and Frying

  1. Roll the Dough: Give the rested dough a quick knead. Divide it into 5-6 equal portions. Roll each portion into a smooth ball.
  2. Cut the Disc: Roll the ball into an oval or circle about 6 inches long and medium-thin (about 2mm thickness). Cut the oval into two semi-circles using a knife.
  3. Form the Cone: Take one semi-circle. Wet the straight edge lightly with water. Take the two corners of the straight edge and bring them together, overlapping them slightly, to form a cone. Press the seam firmly to seal the cone.
  4. Fill and Seal: Hold the cone open. Fill it about three-quarters full with the cooled potato filling (approx. 1.5 - 2 tablespoons).
  5. Pinch and Seal: Wet the remaining open edges of the cone. Gently pinch a pleat/fold on one side of the seam near the sealed point. Press the open edges together firmly to create the final triangle shape.
  6. Fry (The Crucial Step): Heat oil in a deep pot over low heat.24 The oil should be just warm, around $300^\circ\text{F}$ ($150^\circ\text{C}$).
  7. Fry on Low: Carefully drop the samosas into the warm oil (do not overcrowd). Maintain low heat. Fry for 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally. The low heat allows the crust to cook through and become flaky without burning.
  8. Finish: Once the samosas are golden brown and crispy, remove them, drain excess oil on paper towels, and serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind chutney.
